Art Therapy and Iatrogenic Alogenosis On the Frontiers of the Self

Adrian, F., Simon, A. (2018).
Ed. Academic Spanish Editorial. Latvia, European Union.

SUMMARY

The purpose of this research was to develop an intervention program based on Art Therapy to adapt the functions of the ego in patients with iatrogenic allogenosis caused by the use of fillers (Biopolymers) attending the non-profit Foundation “No to Biopolymers , Yes to Life ”(San Diego, Carabobo State). In this sense, the theoretical postulates considered to be the basis are framed in Art Therapy, the egos Functions and Iatrogenic Alogenosis; Thus, for the fulfillment of the objective set, the study relied on a quasi-experimental field design, developed in 4 phases and with an explanatory level. The population consisted of 7 women from 20 to 50 years old, to whom the data collection instrument Assessment of the functions of the I (EFY) of Bellak & Goldsmith (1989) was applied; in relation to the data analysis technique, the results were obtained from the application of the pre-test and post-test compared by Student's t-statistical formula; analyzed qualitatively and quantitatively, obtaining a bilateral significance greater than 0.05; for the levels of adequacy of the egos functions, thus checking the null hypothesis, that is, that Art Therapy could not adapt the Functions of the ego in patients with Iatrogenic Allogenosis caused by the use of fillers (Biopolymers).

Descriptors: Art therapy, functions of the ego, Iatrogenic Allogenosis, Biopolymers
